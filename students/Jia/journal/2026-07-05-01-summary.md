## Tutor feedback

🌟 Two things you did well:
- **Clear order words again** — "First… Then… In the end of today's part…" Your structure is dependable now; the reader always knows where they are in the story. 🏗️
- **You caught a subtle detail** — "Harry noticed that Sirius Black was first really happy for him but became moodier and surlier than before." That's not just plot — that's noticing how a *character changes*. Really sharp reading! 🔍

🔧 One thing to level up (structure — your focus skill): Your "Then" sentence tries to do three things at once (Harry cleared + everyone happy + Sirius's mood change), which makes it a long run-on. Try splitting it and using a **fourth order word**, like:
   "Then, Harry got his name cleared and everyone at Sirius's house was happy. **After that,** Harry noticed that Sirius seemed happy at first but slowly became moodier and talked less."
   That gives you a clean First → Then → After that → In the end. Easier to follow!

🚀 Stretch challenge (+10 XP, optional): Add a **forward-looking closing sentence** — "Now that Harry is cleared, he has to ______." Ending a summary by pointing ahead is a Tier 2 move!
