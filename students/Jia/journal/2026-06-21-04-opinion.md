## Tutor feedback

🌟 Two things you did well:
- You started with a **clear opinion** right away — "In my opinion, Black is really mean." A reader knows exactly what you think from the very first words.
- You backed it up with **real evidence from the book** — "Black knew where their secret keeper was and he told Voldemort about it." That's a specific detail, not just a feeling. 💪

🔧 One thing to level up: **end punctuation.** Your last sentence is missing its period. Every sentence needs an ending mark so the reader knows where to stop. Yours would become: "That's why I think Black is really mean**.**" Small fix, big difference!

🚀 Stretch challenge (+10 XP, optional): Right now your ending repeats your opening exactly ("Black is really mean" twice). Try making your **last sentence add a NEW thought** — like how Black's betrayal makes Harry feel, or why betraying a *friend* is even worse than just being mean.
