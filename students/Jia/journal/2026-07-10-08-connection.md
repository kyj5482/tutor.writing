## My writing

In the book, Harry got banned from the Gryffindor Quidditch team. This reminds me of my friend who got from the group chat. They are alike because Harry and my friend both got banned.

## Tutor feedback

🌟 Two things you did well:
- You picked a clear book event AND a real personal connection — Harry banned from Quidditch, your friend left out of the group chat. That's a genuine, relatable link!
- You kept the order clean: book event → your connection → "alike because." Beginning, middle, end — nice structure. 🎯

🔧 One thing to level up (this week's focus — Explanation): Your "alike because" says they both got banned, but that just repeats the facts. Push to the *feeling* — the "so what?" Try: "They are alike because Harry and my friend both got kicked out of something they loved, and being left out like that feels unfair and lonely." Now the "because" explains WHY the connection matters.

🚀 Stretch challenge (+10 XP, optional): Add a sentence about how they're DIFFERENT, then finish with "I understand Harry better because…". Example: "But they're different because my friend got added back, and Harry is still banned. I understand Harry better now — being left out with no way back would make me angry, just like him." That's a Tier 2 move!
