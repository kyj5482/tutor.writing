# 2026-07-04 — Letter to a Character

- **Book:** Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (about 100 pages)
- **Template:** 05 Letter to a Character, Tier 1
- **XP earned:** 25 (base 10 + focus skill "structure" 5 + ACE bonus 10)

## My writing

Dear Harry, I saw that you moved to Sirius Black's house from Privet Drive and hearing stuff about Voldemort. I think you should understand that Dumbledore has his reason not to tell you everything about Voldemort because you might freak out once you find out what is really going on with Voldemort. Your friend, Jia

## Tutor feedback

🌟 Two things you did well:
- **You reacted to a real, specific event** — "you moved to Sirius Black's house from Privet Drive and hearing stuff about Voldemort." You named exact places (Privet Drive, Sirius's house), not just "stuff happened." 🎯
- **Your advice comes with a real reason** — "you should understand that Dumbledore has his reasons... *because* you might freak out." That "because" makes your advice feel wise and caring, not random. 🏗️

🔧 One thing to level up (structure — your focus skill): Your letter jumps straight from the *event* to *advice* — try adding a **middle sentence about how YOU feel** about what's happening to Harry. That gives your letter a real beginning → middle → end. For example: "When you had to leave and no one would tell you anything, I felt frustrated for you." Then your advice lands even stronger.

🚀 Stretch challenge (+10 XP, optional): Add a **"reader secret"** — one thing you know (as the reader) that Harry doesn't know yet, teased without spoiling: "Be careful about ______…" It makes the letter feel like you know more than he does!
