## Tutor feedback

🌟 Two things you did well:
- **Amazing recall and specific details** — you packed in real moments from the book like the invisibility cloak, the midnight meeting with Hagrid, and Black's warning about Karkaroff. That's a LOT of true detail from your reading. 📚
- **You added a creative "what if" twist** — "But what if Malfoy was the one who put Harry's name?" Malfoy already dislikes Harry, so it's a twist that really fits the story. 🪄

🔧 One thing to level up (structure — your focus skill): Your "what if" twist and its results got **split apart**. You start the twist ("what if Malfoy...") at the beginning, but Malfoy getting caught and expelled lands all the way at the end, after the real events. Try keeping your twist and what-happens-because-of-it **together**, like:
   "But what if Malfoy was the one who put Harry's name? Then Malfoy would get caught by Dumbledore and Harry. And in the end, Malfoy would get expelled once everyone finds out."
   That way the "what if" story flows start → middle → end in one piece.

🚀 Stretch challenge (+10 XP, optional): Add **one line of dialogue** with quotation marks — like Dumbledore catching Malfoy: *"Mr. Malfoy, did you put Harry's name in the Goblet?"* One spoken line makes a scene feel alive!
