## My writing

Dear Harry, I saw that you saw Mr. Weasley getting attacked by snake today. I think you should just try not to think about Voldemort since it is going to freak you out more once you think about him. Your friend, Jia

## Tutor feedback

🌟 Two things you did well:
- Your advice came WITH a reason — "try not to think about Voldemort since it is going to freak you out more." That "since" is exactly this week's Explanation focus: advice + a because. In your own words! 🎯
- You reacted to the real event and wrote a true letter — "Dear Harry" at the top, "Your friend, Jia" at the bottom, all about the actual snake vision.

🔧 One thing to level up: Add how YOU felt when you read the scary part — and explain why. Right now you jump straight to advice; a great letter also shows the writer's heart. Try adding a line like: "When you saw Mr. Weasley get hurt, I felt scared for you because the vision seemed so real."

🚀 Stretch challenge (+10 XP, optional): Add a "reader tease" — one thing YOU know that Harry doesn't yet, without spoiling it. Example: "Be careful about trusting everything you see in your head, Harry — those visions might not be as simple as they look." That's a Tier 2 letter move!

**Question:** Why is it so strange and scary that Harry could SEE Mr. Weasley being attacked, even though he wasn't there?

It is scary and strange that Harry could see Mr. Weasley being attacked, even though he wasn't their because it is not normal. In the book, Harry was scared that he might be getting controlled by Voldemort. This shows that Harry is scared because he was the snake that attacked Mr. Weasley.
