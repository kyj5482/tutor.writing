## My writing

Would you give up your life, home, and friends to be in the 39 clue hunt? Amy and Dan choose the 39 clues over everything. They are now on the hunt and they are in the Caribbean, where the next clue is located. They have a major problem, the Madrigals.

The book does a incredible job in showing the event in 1st person and 3rd person view. It gives us a little bit of heads up, and it really helps with the action part. "While Ian Kabra is watching the Cahills...." it is a clear example of 3rd person view.

I think it deserves a full 5 star because the plot and the action is just excellent. If you liked Keeper of the lost cities you should definitely read this book, it's perfect.

## Tutor feedback

🌟 **Two things you did well:**
1. Your **hook** — *"Would you give up your life, home, and friends to be in the 39 clue hunt?"* — is a real question that names three specific stakes AND hints at the book's heart, exactly like a strong Tier-3 opener. It pulls the reader right in.
2. Your **craft observation** was sharp: you noticed the book shifts between **1st person and 3rd person** view *and* backed it up with a quote you found yourself — *"While Ian Kabra is watching the Cahills...."* That's sophisticated reading, and using the quote as proof is a Tier-3 move.

🔧 **One thing to level up (verb check):** *"the plot and the action **is** just excellent."* When two things are joined by **and**, they act as a plural — so it needs **are**: *"the plot and the action **are** just excellent."* Reading that last sentence out loud before saving would catch it every time.
