## Tutor feedback

🌟 Two things you did really well:
- **You showed a feeling with your body AND made it honest.** "I stood still like a statue" freezes the moment, and then you went deeper: "I should be glad that there are one less person to compete. But, something inside me stopped me." That tug-of-war between what you *should* feel and what you *actually* feel is the beating heart of a diary.
- **You used a precise, close-reading detail to drive the action.** "I saw Hamilton sending me a morse code." That specific detail (a Holt secretly helping!) turns the whole scene and proves you read closely.

🔧 One thing to level up (verb-check focus): Read this aloud — "...me and my sister **was** out of here!" The subject is two people, so the verb needs to be plural: **were**. (A read-aloud pass catches one more of the same kind: "I could have **ran** away" → "could have **run** away." Same skill.)

🚀 Stretch challenge (+10 XP): You're writing as Dan — a wisecracking, snack-loving 11-year-old. Add one line in HIS actual voice: a joke, a sarcastic aside, or a secret thought he'd never say out loud. That's a Tier 3 "match the character's voice" move.
