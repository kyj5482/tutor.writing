## My writing

My golden line: "You kids took your sweet time getting here!"

## Tutor feedback

🌟 **Two things you did well:**
1. **The zoom-in.** You noticed "You kids" is "Mr. Forkle's favorite word to start with." Recognizing a character by their *signature phrase* is exactly the Tier 3 move — you zoomed into the words, not just the whole line.
2. **The connection.** You realized that *by hearing this sentence* Sophie and her friends know "they reached the hideout of the black swan." That's sharp — you saw the line itself works as a signal, a clue that tells them they made it. The words *are* the confirmation.

🔧 **One thing to level up (verb check):** "Sophie and her friends **knows**" → **know**. The subject is plural (Sophie *and* her friends = "they"), so the verb drops the ‑s.
   - Fixed: "Sophie and her friends **know** that they reached the hideout of the Black Swan."

🚀 **Stretch challenge (+10 XP, optional):** Go one layer deeper on the zoom-in. You said "You kids" is Mr. Forkle's word — now ask *why the author gives him that gruff, teasing line instead of a warm "Welcome, you made it."* What does "took your sweet time" tell us about who Mr. Forkle *is* to Sophie and her friends?
