## My writing

How would you feel if you have no control over your own life and has to go through a test that a company made for you. The Scorch Trials by James Dashner shows exactly what it would be like. Thomas who was thrown in the Maze by WICKED, had no choice but to risk his life and fight the Grievers in order to escape. Only to find that the Maze was only trial 1 and his group has to pass the Scorch Trials to get the cure for their disease, the Flare.
        This book does an excellent job on how does our main character, Thomas feels and describing the situations. But on the other hand, the ending was really bad because it was basically reaping itself from the beging.
        It loses 1 star because of the ending but it earns the 4 point because this book was so creative about the whole trial and the describing what Thomas goes through. If you liked The Hunger Games but want more challenge and emotional feeling, this book is the perfect fit for you.

1. Killer hook — "How would you feel if you have no control over your own life and has to go through a test that a company made for you." You start with a question that pulls the reader in, exactly like a real book review.

🔧 **One thing to level up — smooth, parallel phrasing.** A couple of sentences get tangled: "does an excellent job on how does our main character, Thomas feels and describing the situations." The "how does" doesn't fit there, and "Thomas feels / describing" mix two different forms. Try parallel verbs: *"This book does an excellent job of **showing** how Thomas feels and **describing** the situations he's in."* (Also: "reaping" → "repeating," "beging" → "beginning.")
