## My writing

In the book, Amy and Dan got the document easily because they were Grace's grandchildren. But what if they were not.

They went inside the underground vault to see the document. But the guard crossed his arms and said "No one is allowed to look at the document except Mrs. Cahill's direct descendants." "Hey, look tough guy. We are part of Cahill which means we are all related." Dan said in a loud voice. "Oh. My bad so, you are related to Mrs. Cahill. Right?" Asked the guard. "Y-y-yeah." Amy stuttered. "Ok. You may enter." The guard said.

Dan and Amy finished reading the document when Grace's actual grandchildren showed up.

## Tutor feedback

🌟 **Two things you did well:**
1. **Amy's stutter** — *"Y-y-yeah." Amy stuttered.* This is PERFECT Amy. In the books she stutters exactly when she's nervous, and you made us *feel* her panic without ever writing "Amy was scared." That's show-don't-tell — a Tier 3 voice move sneaking into your writing.
2. **Dan's bluff** — *"Hey, look tough guy. We are part of Cahill which means we are all related."* That's 100% Dan: loud, sarcastic, and sneaky-smart. The loophole logic (all Cahills are technically related!) is clever. And you kept the whole scene in steady past tense — verb-check focus ✅.

🔧 **One thing to level up:** Your last line — *"Dan and Amy finished reading the document when Grace's actual grandchildren showed up"* — is a great cliffhanger idea, but right now you *tell* it, so it reads like a summary. End ON the live moment instead. Example using your scene:
> *Just as Dan read the last line, the vault door creaked open behind them. "Those aren't Grace's grandchildren," a cold voice said. "We are."*
See how ending mid-action, with a voice, makes your heart jump? Now you try rewriting your last line that way.

🚀 **Stretch challenge (+10 XP, optional):** Add ONE show-don't-tell body-language detail (a Tier 3 move). When the guard first stops them, show Amy's fear with her body *before* she stutters — like *"Amy's fingers tightened around Dan's sleeve."* One small action that shows the feeling.
