🌟 **Two things you did well:**
1. Your **topic sentence does its whole job at once** — it names the book, the author, AND the big event: "James Dashner shows the deal between Thomas and Jorge, the leader of the Cranks." A reader knows exactly what's coming.
2. Your **details are specific and accurate** — Jorge, Brenda, the cure to the Flare, WICKED, the "gone." You summarized SIX chapters without it turning into "and then, and then." Transitions like "When…," "Later…" kept the order clear. That's solid Tier-2 structure and evidence. 💪

🔧 **One thing to level up (Explanation — your focus skill):** Your closing sentence names the topics ("the ongoing conflict… and the separation of the Gladers"), but those are still *events*. The level-up is to say *why* that matters — what's now at stake for Thomas.
   - Your sentence: *"What matters about these chapters is… the separation of the Gladers."*
   - With a why: *"What matters about these chapters is the separation of the Gladers, **because** now Thomas has to trust Brenda — a stranger — instead of the group he survived the Maze with."*
   See how adding "because…" turns a fact into meaning? Try rewriting just your last sentence that way.
