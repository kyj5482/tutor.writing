# Grade 6 Examples — *Hatchet* by Gary Paulsen

All examples below imagine a 6th grader who read **chapters 1–3** of *Hatchet*: Brian
Robeson, 13, is flying in a tiny plane to visit his dad in Canada. His parents just
divorced and he carries a painful secret about his mom. The pilot has a heart attack
mid-flight, and Brian has to crash-land the plane into a lake — alone.

Examples are written at **Tier 2** unless marked. They're good, not perfect — that's
the point. Your writing should sound like YOU.

---

## 01 — Summary

Today I read the first three chapters of *Hatchet*, where Brian's trip turns into a
nightmare. First, Brian gets on a small Cessna plane to visit his dad in Canada,
and he keeps thinking about his parents' divorce and "The Secret" he knows about
his mom. After that, the pilot suddenly grabs his chest and has a heart attack,
and Brian is left flying a plane he doesn't know how to fly. He tries the radio
but he loses the signal. Finally, when the fuel runs out, Brian aims the plane at
a lake and crashes into the water. Now Brian is alone in the wilderness with
nothing but the hatchet his mom gave him.

## 02 — Prediction

I predict that Brian will figure out how to get food from the lake, like fish.
One clue is that he crashed right next to a lake, and authors don't put a lake
there for no reason. Another clue is that the book is called *Hatchet*, so I think
he will use the hatchet to make tools, maybe a spear. My confidence is 8 out of
10, because Brian seems smart — he stayed calm enough to aim the plane at the
water instead of the trees.

## 03 — Character Diary

Dear Diary,
I still can't believe what happened today. One minute the pilot was showing me
the controls, and the next minute he was screaming and grabbing his arm. My heart
was slamming so hard I could hear it over the engine. I kept pushing the radio
button and saying "Help, I need help," but the voice on the other end faded out.
I'm worried that nobody knows where the plane turned, so the searchers will look
in the wrong place. But I hope my dad notices I'm late and calls someone. I made
it into the lake. I'm alive. That has to count for something.
— Brian

## 04 — Opinion / Response

I think Brian's mom should not have given him a hatchet as a present, but it's
going to save his life anyway. My evidence is that when she gave it to him, Brian
felt embarrassed and didn't even want to wear it on his belt. This shows that the
gift didn't fit who Brian was in the city — but in the wilderness, it's the only
tool he has. That's why I think the hatchet is a weird present that turns out to
be the best present.

## 05 — Letter to a Character

Dear Brian,
When the pilot had the heart attack and you grabbed the controls, I felt scared
for you, but I was also kind of proud. You should keep talking out loud to
yourself because it seems to keep you calm. I have two questions for you: Why
didn't you tell your dad about The Secret? And do you really think the divorce
was your fault? Also, be careful — I know something you don't. The radio is not
going to be your way out.
Your friend, Mina

## 06 — Rewrite the Scene (What If…?)

In the book, the radio signal fades out before Brian can tell anyone where he is.
But what if the radio had worked for one more minute?

"Cessna four-six, say your heading," the voice crackled.
"I don't know!" Brian shouted. "The pilot is — he's not moving. There's lakes
everywhere. Just lakes and trees!"
"Stay calm, son. Look at the dial in the middle. Read me the number."
Brian wiped his eyes and leaned forward. The needle trembled at 342. "Three-four-
two!" he yelled. Then the static swallowed everything again.
He didn't know it yet, but somewhere far behind him, a man wrote that number down.

## 07 — Golden Line

**My golden line:** "He was alone. In the roaring plane with no pilot he was
alone. Alone." (chapter 1)

In the story, this is right after the pilot's heart attack, when Brian realizes
no one is going to help him. I think the author repeats the word "alone" three
times on purpose, like the word is echoing in Brian's head. The sentences get
shorter and shorter, which makes me read faster, like panic. It makes me wonder
how long Brian will stay alone — the whole book?

## 08 — Connection

In the book, Brian keeps replaying the moment he saw his mom's secret, and it
makes the divorce hurt even more. This reminds me of when my best friend moved
away last year and I kept replaying our last day at the pool over and over.
They're alike because we both couldn't stop thinking about something we couldn't
change. But they're different too, because Brian's secret makes him angry at his
mom, and my memory just made me sad, not mad. I think I understand Brian better
now — when he snaps at the memory of his mother, it's not because he's mean, it's
because thinking about it hurts.

## 09 — Quick Write 3-2-1 (for a short-reading day)

**3 things that happened:**
1. Brian boarded the small plane and the pilot let him try the controls.
2. The pilot had a heart attack and Brian had to take over.
3. Brian crash-landed the plane into a lake and swam out.

**2 words:** "rudder" — I think it's the part that steers the plane, because the
pilot moves it to turn. "Wincing" — I guess it means making a face from pain,
because the pilot does it when his arm hurts.

**1 question:** Will the plane sink with all the supplies inside? My guess is yes,
and that's why the hatchet on Brian's belt is going to matter so much.

## 10 — Book Review *(example written as if the whole book was finished)*

Could you survive 54 days alone in the Canadian wilderness with only a hatchet?
*Hatchet* by Gary Paulsen is about 13-year-old Brian, whose plane crashes in the
middle of nowhere while he's flying to visit his dad. No food, no shelter, no
people — just mosquitoes and a hatchet.

The best thing about this book is that you feel every mistake Brian makes, like
when he eats the wrong berries. The chapters are short and almost every one ends
with a problem, so it's really hard to stop reading. One thing that could be
better is that some parts about the divorce repeat a little too much. I give it
4.5 out of 5 stars. If you liked *My Side of the Mountain* or any survival show,
you'll love this book.
