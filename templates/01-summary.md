# 01 — Summary

**Skill:** main idea, sequencing, choosing what matters
**Best when:** you read about a chapter or more

## Tier 1 (4–5 sentences)

Fill in the frame:

- Today I read about ______ (who) ______ (doing what).
- First, ______.
- Then, ______.
- In the end of today's part, ______.

✅ Checklist: character names (not just "he"), 1 specific detail, end punctuation.

## Tier 2 (1 solid paragraph, 6–8 sentences)

- Start with a **topic sentence** that names the book and the big thing that happened.
- Tell the events **in order** using transitions: *first, after that, then, finally*.
- Include **2–3 specific details** (names, places, numbers, objects).
- End with how today's part left things: *Now ______ has to ______.*

✅ Checklist: topic sentence, 3+ transitions, no "and then and then and then".

## Tier 3 (1–2 paragraphs)

- Summarize **only what matters** — skip small stuff. Ask: would the story break
  without this event? If no, cut it.
- Include **one short quote** (even 3–4 words) that captures the moment.
- End with a sentence about **why this part matters** to the whole story.

✅ Checklist: nothing retold word-by-word, a quote, a "why it matters" closer.
