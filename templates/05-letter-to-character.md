# 05 — Letter to a Character

**Skill:** audience awareness, empathy, advice with reasons
**Best when:** you want to warn, thank, comfort, or argue with a character

Write a letter TO a character about what happened in today's reading. Start with
*Dear ______,* and sign it with your name.

## Tier 1 (4–5 sentences)

- Dear ______,
- I saw that you ______ today.
- I think you should ______ because ______.
- Your friend, ______

✅ Checklist: letter format, one piece of advice with a "because".

## Tier 2 (1 paragraph letter)

- React to the specific event: *When you ______, I felt ______.*
- Give advice OR ask the character 2 honest questions.
- Tell them one thing you know (as the reader) that they don't know yet — without
  spoiling, tease it: *Be careful about ______…*

✅ Checklist: reacts to a real event, advice/questions, reader-knowledge moment.

## Tier 3 (2 short paragraphs)

- Match your tone to the relationship: are you writing as a friend, a coach, a
  worried parent? Keep that voice consistent.
- Use one short quote of something the character said and respond to it directly:
  *You said "______" — but do you really believe that?*
- End with a P.S. that shows personality.

✅ Checklist: consistent tone, quoted line answered, a P.S.
