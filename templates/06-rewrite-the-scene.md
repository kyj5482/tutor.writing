# 06 — Rewrite the Scene (What If…?)

**Skill:** creativity inside plot logic, narrative writing
**Best when:** you read a big scene and thought "I would have done it differently"

Pick one moment from today's reading and change ONE thing: a decision, a line of
dialogue, who walks in the door. Then write what happens instead.

## Tier 1 (4–6 sentences)

- In the book, ______ happened.
- But what if ______ instead?
- Then, ______.
- And in the end, ______.

✅ Checklist: states the real event first, then the change, then 2+ consequences.

## Tier 2 (1–2 paragraphs, written as a story)

- Write it as a **scene**, not a summary — include at least 2 lines of dialogue
  with quotation marks.
- Keep the characters acting like themselves. (Would they really say that?)
- End at a mini-cliffhanger.

✅ Checklist: dialogue punctuated correctly, characters in-character, cliffhanger.

## Tier 3 (2–3 paragraphs)

- Match the **author's style** — sentence length, mood, the kind of details they
  notice. Your scene should feel like a missing page from the real book.
- Show feelings through **action and body language**, not labels ("His hands
  wouldn't stop moving" instead of "He was nervous").
- Your change should ripple: show one consequence the character doesn't see coming.

✅ Checklist: reads like the book, show-don't-tell, a hidden consequence.
